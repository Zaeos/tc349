Interpretations

My interpretation of the architecture is, I feel, fairly straightforward. The more "business" side of the site is grouped together and the information about the product are all strictly related to the main purpose of the site. The site information is different in nature than either of these because it is all relevant to the site itself, not necessarily to the product. The blog also seems to be its own separate entity, and so it is alone in a group.

Derrick split the content into groups similarly but more broken up than my own. His grouping is more specific, so as to keep styling distinct and isolated.

Jim had the category sales tools, similar to my e-commerce. Customer Interaction is a fairly intuitive category as well.

Karl viewed the site more as an interactive process than a proper site layout. Perhaps here the more "fun" aspects of the site can be isolated from the more business-like.
